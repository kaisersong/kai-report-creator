Analytics Script

The team uses the following Python script to pull per-article performance data from the CMS analytics API, compute a composite engagement score, and surface the top candidates for repurposing. It runs nightly via a GitHub Actions cron job and posts a digest to the #content-metrics Slack channel.

Content Performance Analyzer — analyzer.py
"""
Content Performance Analyzer
Fetches article metrics from the CMS API, computes engagement scores,
and returns a ranked list of top-performing and optimization-candidate pieces.
"""

from __future__ import annotations

import os
import json
import datetime
from dataclasses import dataclass, field
from typing import Optional
import urllib.request
import urllib.parse


CMS_API_BASE = os.environ.get("CMS_API_BASE", "https://cms.example.com/api/v2")
CMS_API_KEY  = os.environ.get("CMS_API_KEY", "")


@dataclass
class ArticleMetrics:
    article_id: str
    title: str
    channel: str
    publish_date: str
    reads: int
    read_through_rate: float   # 0.0 – 1.0
    social_shares: int
    email_opens: int = 0
    score: float = field(init=False)

    def __post_init__(self) -> None:
        # Composite engagement score: weighted sum of normalized signals
        self.score = round(
            self.reads * 0.40
            + self.read_through_rate * 10_000 * 0.30
            + self.social_shares * 2.5 * 0.20
            + self.email_opens * 1.5 * 0.10,
            2,
        )


def fetch_articles(
    since: Optional[str] = None,
    channel: Optional[str] = None,
    limit: int = 100,
) -> list[dict]:
    """Retrieve articles from the CMS analytics API."""
    params: dict[str, str] = {"limit": str(limit), "sort": "publish_date:desc"}
    if since:
        params["published_after"] = since
    if channel:
        params["channel"] = channel

    url = f"{CMS_API_BASE}/articles/metrics?{urllib.parse.urlencode(params)}"
    req = urllib.request.Request(
        url,
        headers={"Authorization": f"Bearer {CMS_API_KEY}", "Accept": "application/json"},
    )
    with urllib.request.urlopen(req, timeout=15) as resp:
        return json.loads(resp.read())["articles"]


def analyze(
    since: Optional[str] = None,
    channel: Optional[str] = None,
    top_n: int = 10,
) -> dict[str, list[ArticleMetrics]]:
    """Return top performers and optimization candidates."""
    raw = fetch_articles(since=since, channel=channel)
    metrics = [
        ArticleMetrics(
            article_id=a["id"],
            title=a["title"],
            channel=a["channel"],
            publish_date=a["published_at"][:10],
            reads=a["reads"],
            read_through_rate=a["read_through_rate"],
            social_shares=a["social_shares"],
            email_opens=a.get("email_opens", 0),
        )
        for a in raw
    ]
    ranked = sorted(metrics, key=lambda m: m.score, reverse=True)
    return {
        "top_performers":    ranked[:top_n],
        "needs_improvement": [m for m in ranked if m.read_through_rate < 0.35][:top_n],
        "generated_at":      datetime.datetime.utcnow().isoformat() + "Z",
    }


if __name__ == "__main__":
    # Example: analyze all Blog articles published in 2024
    report = analyze(since="2024-01-01", channel="blog", top_n=5)
    print(f"Report generated: {report['generated_at']}\n")
    print("Top Performers:")
    for i, article in enumerate(report["top_performers"], 1):
        print(f"  {i}. [{article.score:>9.1f}] {article.title[:60]} ({article.reads:,} reads)")
    print("\nArticles That Need Improvement (read-through < 35%):")
    for article in report["needs_improvement"]:
        print(f"  - {article.title[:60]} ({article.read_through_rate:.0%} RTR)")

Editorial Notices

Beyond growth metrics, responsible and sustainable publishing requires clear guidelines on data accuracy, platform dynamics, and legal compliance. The following four notices reflect lessons learned in 2024 and standing policy for 2025.

📋
Data Methodology: All read counts in this report are deduplicated unique pageviews from Google Analytics 4, normalized with a 30-second minimum engagement threshold to filter bot traffic. Read-through rate is derived from scroll-depth tracking (80% scroll = "read"). Social engagement figures aggregate likes, shares, comments, and saves across all tracked platforms. Data is refreshed daily at 02:00 UTC.
💡
Best-Performing Content Type: Data consistently shows that long-form how-to guides published on Tuesday or Wednesday between 09:00 and 11:00 local audience time outperform all other content types and time slots by an average of 22% on read-through rate and 35% on social shares. Articles that combine original research data, actionable frameworks, and real-world case studies perform best. Prioritize this format for new cornerstone pieces.
⚠️
Platform Algorithm Change — LinkedIn & Newsletter: In Q4 2024, LinkedIn reduced organic reach for links in post body text by an estimated 40%. Going forward, publish the full article excerpt natively on LinkedIn with the link in the first comment. For email newsletters, inbox providers have tightened image-to-text ratio rules; maintain at least 60% text content by character count to avoid the Promotions tab. Monitor open rates closely for any month-over-month drops exceeding 5pp.
🚫
Copyright Compliance — Mandatory: All images, data charts, and quoted statistics must have documented licensing before publication. Stock photos require a valid license on file; Creative Commons assets must be attributed per license terms. Reproducing more than 50 words verbatim from any third-party source requires explicit written permission. Failure to comply may result in DMCA takedowns, platform penalties, or legal liability. The compliance review step in the editorial workflow is not optional and cannot be bypassed under any deadline pressure.

Image Example

High-quality cover images are the first checkpoint for driving click-through. Based on 2024 A/B test results, warm-toned covers (amber / sienna / cream) outperformed cool-toned alternatives by approximately 23% on click-through rate on both the Blog and Newsletter — fully aligned with the warm-editorial brand identity.

The design team maintains a unified cover specification: primary color #B45309, Merriweather bold typeface, approximately 40:60 text-to-image ratio, and generous white space to ensure thumbnail readability across platforms. All covers must be reviewed by a designer and uploaded to the shared asset library before entering the publication queue.

For YouTube thumbnails, the standard is 1280×720px with a minimum font size of 36pt for any text overlay. Contrast ratio between text and background must meet WCAG AA (4.5:1) to maximize accessibility and legibility at small display sizes.

Year in Review

For the Content Growth Team, 2024 was a genuine inflection point. We stopped optimizing purely for volume and began asking harder questions about depth, durability, and the kind of trust that compels a reader to return — not because an algorithm pushed a notification, but because the last piece was worth their time.

The year tested our adaptability. LinkedIn's algorithm changes in Q4 forced a rethink of distribution mechanics almost overnight. The rise of AI-generated content in our topic areas raised the bar for originality. And the rapid maturation of short-video formats meant that ignoring YouTube was no longer a viable editorial stance. Each disruption pushed us to clarify what we actually stand for as a publication.

The deepest editorial insight of 2024: The ultimate competitive advantage in content marketing is not producing more content — it is building the kind of trust that makes readers seek you out. Metrics can quantify reads, but they cannot capture the moment a reader finishes your article at midnight and quietly hits subscribe.

Standing at the beginning of 2025 and looking back, we have distilled the following cross-cutting lessons. We offer them to any content team navigating a similarly turbulent landscape:

  • Depth never goes out of style
    • Short content drives distribution; long content builds trust — both are necessary and neither replaces the other
    • A single rigorous deep-dive typically delivers more high-intent subscribers than ten trend-chasing posts
  • Data is a compass, not a cage
    • Use data to surface opportunity, but do not let data override editorial judgment on what matters to your audience
    • Read-through rate and bookmarks are far better leading indicators of long-term value than raw page views
  • The team is the product
    • A consistent publishing rhythm depends on clear processes and genuine autonomy, not relentless pressure
    • The departure of two senior editors mid-year had a more lasting impact on output quality than any single algorithm change

In 2025, our focus areas are AI-assisted research workflows, a premium reader community, and measured brand content commercialization. Whatever tools evolve, the commitment to writing things worth reading will remain the foundation of everything we publish.
